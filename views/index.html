Intro

To the factory owners who still use fossil fuels, the world we live in has gone to despair. We are experiencing the effect of greenhouse gasses and industrial activities—global warming. Climate change is an enduring issue and still is a current problem because of human activities, and we, as humanity, need to end it. It’s always been an issue since the Industrial Revolution(1760), that’s almost 3 centuries ago (260 years)! Burning fossil fuels, in combination with the destruction of carbon sinks due to deforestation and other activities such as pollution are a problem, but can be solved using methods such as renewable energy. However, some might say that this isn’t the fault of humanity, and it’s natural—the earth’s climate is always changing like this. So, what will happen?

Burning Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuel coal had been used as a fuel since 1,000 B.C., it wasn't until the arrival of the Industrial Revolution from the mid-1700s through the 1800s that coal began to replace biomass as the primary source of energy. In the article “Overview: Weather, Global Warming and Climate Change.” it states, “due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth’s atmosphere”. This explains the cause of using fossil fuels and their impact on our atmosphere. NASA themselves say this is the effect of factory owners who still use fossil fuels. Would you want the end of our world to end just because of some greedy factory owners who care about profit more than the environment? Some people might say this is a natural thing to happen, and humanity has nothing to do with climate change as stated, “Natural processes can also contribute to climate change, including internal variability (…) and external forces (...)” because it demonstrates the possibilities of other factors causing the climate to change, meaning none of what we are doing is affecting it. However, this assumption is incorrect because as also stated, “Changes observed in Earth’s climate since the early 20th century are primarily driven by human activities, particularly fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth’s atmosphere, raising Earth’s average surface temperature. These human-produced temperature increases are commonly referred to as global warming”. This refutes false claims because according to the text “Changes observed in Earth’s climate since the early 20th century are primarily driven by human activities, particularly fossil fuel burning” and promote the idea of experiencing the effect of greenhouse gasses and industrial activities—global warming.

How can Renewable Energy Help?

In the United States, about 29% of global warming emissions come from our electricity sector. According to “Benefits of Renewable Energy Use.”, “Increasing the supply of renewable energy would allow us to replace carbon-intensive energy sources and significantly reduce US global warming emissions”. This demonstrates a solution to global warming by allowing us and factories that use “carbon-intensive energy sources” to replace renewable energy sources. The UCS membership includes many private citizens in addition to professional scientists. This can cause a change in our environment and become a solution because a UCS analysis says “25 percent by 2025 national renewable electricity standards would lower power plant CO2 emissions 277 million metric tons annually by 2025—the equivalent of the annual output from 70 typical (600 MW) new coal plants”. This shows this indeed is possible and doable as long as people cooperate until climate change is no longer an enduring issue.
